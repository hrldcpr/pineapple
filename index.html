My background.

I teach yoga and qi gong because I care about people. In a world that puts profit over human lives and our planet earth, I believe caring for ourselves and for each other is where the mundane meets the sacred.

I started practicing yoga in 2012 shortly after I moved to New York. As I passed through the worlds of academia, children’s books, education, and nonprofits, my practice rooted and oriented me. When my grandmother got sick in 2017, I quit everything else and committed myself to what mattered the most: my family, my practice, and my community.

The more I explore embodiment — what it means and how it feels — the more my life transforms. No, I'm not significantly more flexible or less anxious. But I am much stronger than I used to be. I am more engaged and connected. I am more myself. While I can’t promise this work will be transformative for you, I do hope you will explore its potential with me.
